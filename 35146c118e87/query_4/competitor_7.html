Begin your journey to a fuller understanding of sleep and dreams

On this free short course you will discover the latest scientific research into sleep science and why we sleep and dream. 

The course also offers tips and strategies on how to sleep better and explores the new science of sleep engineering where sleep scientists are finding novel ways to manipulate sleep to maximise its benefits for health and cognition.

The course  features video tutorials from world renowned experts, quizzes, and opportunities to interact with other learners on the course.

What will you learn?

  • What happens when we sleep
  • The various stages of sleep and dreaming 
  • Key theories on why we sleep and dream and the experimental evidence that support these theories 
  • Sleep engineering to reduce cognitive decline, increase memory reactivation and to decouple emotions from memories
  • The potential opportunities for learning in your sleep
  • Tips and strategies to improve your own sleep

Who is this course suitable for?

This course is for anyone with a desire to explore the latest research into sleep science and why we sleep and dream. 

The course also is useful for those interested in discovering tips and strategies on how to sleep better.

Upon completing the course, you will be issued with a digital certificate. This can be used as evidence to show during job and university applications or appraisals. Your digital certificate URL can also be shared socially and added to Linkedin or your CV.

Who are your expert tutors?

Anil Seth

Professor of cognitive and computational neuroscience, University of Sussex

Anil Seth is a professor of cognitive and computational neuroscience at the University of Sussex. Anil’s research seeks to understand the biological basis of consciousness by bringing together research across neuroscience, mathematics, computer science, psychology, philosophy and psychiatry.

As a co-director of the Sackler Centre for Consciousness Science, his work contributes to the centre’s aim of translating an enhanced understanding of the complex brain networks underpinning consciousness into new clinical approaches to psychiatric disorders, and to drive new developments in artificial intelligence and virtual reality.

Anil contributes to a variety of media including New Scientist, The Guardian and the BBC . He was a consultant for Eye Benders, winner of the Royal Society Young People’s Book Prize in 2014 and he writes the blog NeuroBanter. His latest book, Being You: The Inside Story of Your Inner Universe, was published in 2021.

Penny Lewis

Sleep scientist and professor of psychology

Penny Lewis is a sleep scientist and professor of psychology at Cardiff University. She has coined the term sleep engineering to capture the spirit of her lab’s work. She explores ingenious ways to enhance memory, disarm negative emotions, and combat cognitive decline through ageing during sleep. Her book, The Secret World of Sleep explores the latest research into the night time brain to understand the real benefits of sleep. She also hosts the Sleep Science podcast.
