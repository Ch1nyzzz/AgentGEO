Investment opportunities

Dublin continues to position itself as a destination for both private and institutional investors seeking strong long-term value. With a startup ecosystem and a diversifying property market, Ireland’s capital has a unique blend of innovation, and sustained growth potential.

Startup Investments

Dublin is now firmly on the map as a European innovation hub. Its start-up landscape spans across a broad range sectors, from SaaS and MedTech to GreenTech and creative industries, consistently attracting both local and international capital.

Dublin’s entrepreneurial ecosystem is bolstered by a support infrastructure. Initiatives such as Enterprise Ireland’s High Potential Startups (HPSU) programme and the Employment Investment Incentive Scheme (EIIS) provide structured pathways for equity-based investment, with tax efficiencies built in.

Enterprise Ireland plays a pivotal role, offering not only funding but investor-ready portfolios too. The agency also facilitates syndicated investment opportunities through partnerships with VC firms and angel networks.

For investors seeking early-stage engagement, Dublin’s Dogpatch Labs broadcast First Fridays for Startups in partnership with Google for Startups live from Dogpatch Studio.

Further access points for venture capital and syndicate investment include:

  • TechIreland – a national innovation database profiling over 150,000 investment opportunities
  • NDRC (National Digital Research Centre) – offering investor
  • The Angel Investment Network & Spark Crowdfunding – enabling more flexible, early-stage exposure across industries

Real estate investments

Despite a market-wide slowdown in 2024, Ireland’s real estate sector is poised for an upswing. Continued domestic economic growth, underpinned by full employment and wage inflation, is creating forward momentum.

Dublin’s property market is being shaped by both macroeconomic tailwinds and regulatory transformation. The Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD) and ESG-driven investment mandates are accelerating the demand for energy-efficient buildings and responsible development strategies.

According to CBRE, investment trends shaping the market in 2025 include:

  • A bifurcation in the office market, with high demand for modern, sustainable buildings
  • Logistics sector growth, driven by warehousing demand and e-commerce logistics
  • Continued expansion in healthcare, hospitality, and experiential retail
  • A rising urgency around refurbishment and repurposing legacy buildings for sustainability compliance

Demographic growth is also reinforcing pressure on the residential sector, creating investor opportunities in build-to-rent, affordable housing, and PRS (Private Rented Sector) developments.

A connected ecosystem

Ireland’s innovation-first policy environment, strong legal framework, and investor-friendly tax landscape provide for sustainable capital deployment.

Whether seeking a presence in high-growth tech ventures or sustainable real asset strategies, Dublin offers a connected platform with the support of public sector partners and a proven innovation ecosystem.

To explore screened opportunities and investor engagement initiatives, contact: Spark Crowdfunding and Linked Finance.
