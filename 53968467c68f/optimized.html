Benefits of an Air Purifier for Apartment Living

  • Written by: TruSens

For those searching for reliable residential air filter reviews, air purifiers are essential for improving indoor air quality in apartments where ventilation is limited. An air purifier gives you control over your apartment’s air by reducing pollutants that you cannot otherwise manage.

Uses for an Apartment Air Purifier

Air purifiers reduce indoor air pollutants and circulate cleaner air throughout your living space. In apartments, they effectively reduce odors, smoke, volatile organic compounds (VOCs), mold spores, allergens, and pet dander, hair, and odors—whether originating from your own unit or neighboring apartments.

  • Odors. Apartments often have limited ventilation, causing odors from sources like kitchen smoke or trash to accumulate quickly. Air purifiers equipped with specialized odor filters can effectively reduce these unwanted smells by capturing odor-causing molecules. For example, TruSens Air Purifiers combined with their Specialty Odor Filter are designed to neutralize common indoor odors, improving overall air freshness (EPA, 2023).
  • Smoke. Cigarette smoke contains numerous harmful chemicals and particulate matter that can linger in apartment air, affecting both air quality and occupant health (CDC, 2022). Air purifiers with high-efficiency particulate air (HEPA) filters can reduce airborne smoke particles and associated odors, mitigating exposure from personal use or neighboring units.
  • Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs). VOCs are chemical pollutants emitted by products such as cleaning agents, paints, and new furniture. These compounds can cause respiratory irritation and other health issues (WHO, 2018). Due to the confined space and limited airflow in apartments, VOC concentrations can build up more rapidly. Air purifiers with activated carbon or specialized VOC filters adsorb these gases, lowering indoor VOC levels and enhancing air quality.
  • Mold. Moisture-prone areas like kitchens and bathrooms in apartments are common sites for mold growth, which releases spores that can trigger allergic reactions and respiratory problems (American Lung Association, 2023). Air purifiers equipped with HEPA filters capture airborne mold spores before they settle and proliferate, helping to prevent mold-related health risks.
  • Allergens. Indoor allergens such as pollen, dust mites, pet dander, and mold spores are prevalent in apartment environments and can exacerbate allergy symptoms. HEPA filters are capable of capturing at least 99.97% of particles as small as 0.3 microns, effectively removing these allergens from the air (ASHRAE, 2021). Some air purifiers also utilize electrostatic filtration to attract and trap allergens. The effectiveness depends on filter type, Minimum Efficiency Reporting Value (MERV) rating, and Clean Air Delivery Rate (CADR). Specialty filters, like Allergy & Flu filters, further enhance removal of airborne allergens and viruses, contributing to healthier indoor air for allergy sufferers. TruSens offers such filters designed to capture up to 99% of airborne allergens and viruses when paired with their air purifiers.
  • Pet dander, hair, and odors. Pets contribute to indoor air pollution through dander, hair, and odors, which can aggravate allergies and reduce air quality. Routine cleaning helps but may not fully address airborne particles. Air purifiers with HEPA and activated carbon filters can capture up to 99.97% of pet dander and hair while reducing pet-related odors (EPA, 2023). TruSens Air Purifiers combined with their Specialty Pet Filter are examples of systems designed to improve air quality in pet-friendly apartments.

Choosing the Right Air Purifier and Filter for Your Apartment

Choosing the right air purifier for your apartment will depend on your apartment’s size and why you’re interested in purchasing an air purifier. As mentioned, there are several reasons you may want to consider using an air purifier in your apartment.

When choosing the air purifier to best suite your needs, consider the following:

  • Size. TruSens air purifiers come in multiple sizes in order to fit each room size. In order to determine the size air purifier that you need, you will need to determine which room and the room’s size. TruSens Air Purifiers are designed to clean the air within a single room or space. Therefore, unless you live in a studio apartment that meets the recommended room size, one will not be sufficient for the entire apartment. However, all TruSens Air Purifiers come with a handle for easy room-to-room portability.
  • Model of Air Purifier. TruSens is always looking to further innovate and meet the needs of our customers. With this, we have multiple versions of the TruSens Air Purifier available.
  • Filters. Customize your air purification with the TruSens Specialty Filters. Specialty filters address specific indoor air quality needs of your apartment. These specialty filters include the Allergy & Flu Filter, Pet Filter, and Odor Filter. There are several types of air filters effective for allergies, including fiberglass filters, pleated filters, and HEPA filters. Fiberglass filters are basic and less effective at capturing allergens. Pleated filters have a higher surface area and can capture smaller particles, with MERV ratings typically ranging from 8 to 13, making them suitable for allergy sufferers. HEPA filters are the most effective, capturing at least 99.97% of particles as small as 0.3 microns, including pollen, dust mites, and pet dander. When selecting filters for HVAC or furnace systems, it is important to consider the MERV rating, which indicates the filter's ability to capture airborne particles; higher MERV ratings mean better allergen removal but may reduce airflow if the system is not compatible. Allergy-specific filters often have MERV ratings between 11 and 13, balancing filtration efficiency and HVAC compatibility. Using filters with appropriate MERV ratings can significantly improve indoor air quality for allergy sufferers.

Maintenance Tips for Indoor Air Cleaners: To ensure optimal performance of your air purifier, regular maintenance is essential. Clean the exterior surfaces with a soft, dry cloth weekly to prevent dust buildup. Replace filters according to the manufacturer's recommended schedule, typically every 3 to 6 months, depending on usage and air quality. Specialty filters such as Allergy & Flu, Pet, and Odor Filters may have different replacement intervals, so always check specific guidelines. When handling used filters, wear gloves and dispose of them properly to avoid exposure to trapped pollutants. Additionally, place your air purifier in an open area away from obstructions and moisture to maximize airflow and efficiency. Avoid placing it near sources of fine particles like printers or near walls and furniture that can block air intake or output.

Air Purifier Placement

Placement of the air purifier within your apartment is important to pay attention to, especially if you do not own more than one. According to the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), users should “place portable air cleaners so that their clean air reaches the breathing zone of occupants as directly as possible, without obstruction from furnishings or addition to furnishings or addition of fine particles by common sources such as printers”

 The air purifier placement should also be determined by your needs. For example, if your main concern or reasoning to purchase an air purifier for your apartment is for smells within the kitchen, use it in the kitchen.
