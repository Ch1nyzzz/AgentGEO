Prepare for Your Vasectomy

The first step is to ensure you have made a fully informed decision. Please review the information on our site and elsewhere. A vasectomy should be considered permanent contraception: proceed if you feel confident and happy with your choice, and please consider if sperm storage is right for you.

Once your surgery is scheduled, it is important you follow the instructions below. Please also review our post-vasectomy instructions so you know what to expect after the procedure.

We encourage you to reach out with any questions.

Request Information

Patient Instructions

  1. Before the Procedure

    • Avoid alcohol consumption 48 hours prior to the procedure.
    • Avoid the elective use of anti-inflammatories (Ibuprofen, Advil, Motrin, Aleve) 48 hours prior to the procedure.
    • Stay hydrated. Drink at least 2 liters of water the day before your procedure.
    • Feeling unwell? If you are feeling sick, let us know as soon as possible so we can reschedule your appointment. Please note that 48 hours of notice is required for cancellations.
     

    Day of the Procedure

      • Get clean! Take a shower beforehand, and clean your penis and scrotum with soap and water.
      • Do some manscaping! Please shave your pubic hair as best as you can. If you have an electric trimmer, do some light trimming.
      • Do not fast! Eat a normal breakfast or lunch before your procedure.
    • Support is key! Please wear supportive underwear to prevent discomfort. Do not wear boxer shorts as they do not provide the necessary support.
    • Stay relaxed! We recommend bringing your phone and headphones to enjoy something calming during your procedure. We joke with our patients that they won’t get through Stairway to Heaven before we’re done.
    • Arrive early! Please check in at least 15 minutes before your scheduled appointment. Allow extra time for traffic and parking. Patients may park in the paid parking lot adjacent to the clinic or use street pay parking in front of the building.
    • Confirm location! We have two locations, West Broadway and New Westminster. Refer to your appointment details and arrive at the correct location.
    • Consider a driver! While most patients can drive themselves home, having a driver arranged makes for a more relaxed experience. 
     

    When you are in the surgical room, you will not be required to fully undress; you just need to lower your pants, lie back on the table, and relax.

     

    General Blood Thinner Guidelines

    These guidelines are general recommendations only. If you are taking blood thinners for a specific medical condition, please consult your family doctor or prescribing physician before making any changes.

     

    • Apixaban (Eliquis): Please withhold your morning dose on the day of your procedure. You may restart your evening dose on the same day.
    • Rivaroxaban (Xarelto): Please withhold your morning dose on the day of your procedure. You may restart the following morning.
    • Aspirin (low dose): Generally safe. No need to stop prior to the procedure.
    • Warfarin: Please disclose at the time of booking. A brief, no-charge call with our Medical Director will be scheduled for clearance. 
    • Clopidogrel (Plavix): Please disclose at the time of booking. A brief, no-charge call with our Medical Director will be scheduled for clearance.
    • Enoxaparin (Lovenox): Please disclose at the time of booking. A brief, no-charge call with our Medical Director will be scheduled for clearance.
