Travel Health Insurance for Hawaii

Why Is Health Insurance for Hawaii Important for Visitors?

Having health insurance is important for foreign visitors, international students, or parents traveling to Hawaii, where the healthcare cost is very expensive.

⚠️ Without Insurance,
  • Even a routine medical treatment can lead to significant financial strain.
  • The average cost for an emergency room visit in Hawaii can range from $250 to $350, but hospital stays are much more expensive, with the average cost per inpatient day at $2,230.
  • Intensive care unit (ICU) stays such as after a stroke can easily exceed $100,000 and treatment for a heart attack may cost at least $20,000.
  • Diagnostic tests like an echocardiogram typically cost between $1,000 and $3,000 depending on the provider, with some facilities in Hawaii charging over $2,500 for a complete study.

Visitors are not eligible for U.S. government health programs like Medicare or Medicaid, and domestic insurance plans usually require residency or citizenship.

Hawaii offers well-equipped healthcare facilities with reputable hospitals and medical centers, providing quality care across its islands. While not as expansive as mainland states, Hawaii's healthcare system is known for its regional excellence and focus on community wellness.

Cities like Honolulu, Hilo, and Kahului have well-regarded hospitals and medical centers that serve both residents and visitors across the islands. While Hawaii provides quality healthcare services, the cost of medical care can be very high, making visitor medical insurance for Hawaii essential for anyone traveling to the Aloha State.

What Factors Influence the Cost of Visitor Health Insurance in Hawaii?

The visitor health insurance Hawaii cost is influenced by several factors, such as:

  • Age of the Traveler: The visitors insurance Hawaii cost is directly dependent on the age of the traveler. Older travelers pay higher premiums due to the increased risk of medical issues.
  • Medical Maximum Coverage: Plans with higher coverage limits (e.g., $100,000 to $1,000,000) tend to cost more.
  • Type of Insurance: Comprehensive plans (covering a wide range of medical services) are more expensive than fixed benefit plans which have specific limits for each treatment.
  • Duration of Stay: The cost of the travel insurance directly depends upon the length of travel.
  • Deductibles: Plans with higher deductibles are cheaper but require higher out-of-pocket expenses.
  • Add-on Benefits: Additional coverage like for pre-existing conditions or adventure sports increase the premium.

Top 5 Mistakes Travelers Make When Buying Hawaii Travel Insurance

1 Waiting Till the Last Minute to Buy Visitors Insurance
Waiting too long can limit your coverage benefits, especially for acute onset pre-existing condition coverage, which requires early purchase.
2 Buying the Cheapest Plan Without Understanding Policy Limitations

5 Not Reviewing COVID-19 Coverage Details
Ensure your policy covers COVID-19 treatment and hospitalization like any other sickness, especially when traveling to busy destinations like Hawaii.

How Do I Choose the Right Hawaii Visitor Insurance?

Traveling to the USA is exciting, however, it should be noted that the US healthcare system is one of the most expensive in the world. Without adequate visitors medical insurance, a medical emergency can cause very significant financial strain, in the order of tens to even hundreds of thousands of Dollars.

To ensure you have the right health protection, here are key factors to consider when buying Hawaii health insurance for visitors:

1. Medical Coverage

Choose a comprehensive plan for medical emergencies, hospitalization, doctor visits etc.., as it offers better protection when compared to fixed-benefit plans which have limited coverage.

2. High Coverage Limits

Given the high US medical costs, choose a plan with a sufficient coverage of between $100,000 to $500,000 for emergency medical expenses. There are visitors insurance plans in the US which offer coverage of $1 million.

3. Deductibles

Be aware of the deductibles and Co-insurance for the amount you must pay out of pocket before the insurance kicks in. A lower deductible has higher expenses initially, but might save a lot of money in a medical emergency.

4. Emergency Medical Evacuation

Travel health insurance for Hawaii covers emergency evacuation to the nearest suitable facility or repatriation home if needed, ensuring protection in case of severe illness or injury.

5. Pre-Existing Conditions

If you have any pre-existing medical conditions, check if the plan covers acute onset of these ailments and the look-back period if any. Some plans on American Visitor Insurance offer limited coverage for unexpected recurrence of an existing ailment.

6. PPO Network

Some US insurance providers work with a network of hospitals that offer cashless or direct billing services. This is very helpful in avoiding large out-of-pocket expenses particularly during emergencies.
