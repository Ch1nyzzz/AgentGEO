Video conferencing is increasingly becoming popular as businesses embrace working from home programs. Investing in one of the best video conferencing equipment lets you naturally interact with your remote workers, much like in-person meetings.

Compared to the standard webcams for home office and streaming, video conference equipment feature smart cameras with a wide field of view, microphones, and room-filling speakers to clearly see and hear every participant.

Here are the best video conferencing equipment for your small businesses.

FAQ

Q: What Equipment Do You Need for Video Conferencing?

The equipment needed for video conferencing includes a camera, microphone, and speaker. The camera and microphone record the video and audio in the office so that the remote workers can hear and see you, and the speakers allow you to listen to them.

Some equipment combines the camera, microphone, and speaker in an all-in-one audio and video conferencing system, but you can also buy them separately or as a bundle. In addition, you'll also require a computer to connect to the video conference service, such as Zoom, and a display to see the remote participants.

Q: What Are the Minimum System Requirements for Video Conferencing?

You don't need to invest in a powerful computer for video conferencing. The minimum system requirements for video conferencing are a dual-core processor and 4GB RAM. Most Intel Core i3 processors or entry-level AMD chips fit the description.

Q: How Much Does Video Conferencing Equipment Cost?

Video conferencing equipment used to cost a premium, but the prices have been dropping in the last few years. You can now buy an all-in-one video conferencing system for comparatively little.
