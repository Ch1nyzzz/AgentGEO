What exactly is Virtual Reality?

The use of computer technology to create a virtual world is known as virtual reality (VR). Except for conventional user interfaces, virtual reality immerses the user in a perception. So instead of looking at a screen, users are engrossed in and communicating with 3D worlds. Virtual reality refers to the use of high-performance machines and sensorimotor equipment such as headsets and gloves to simulate parts of our world (or completely imaginary worlds).

Augmented reality is evolving and becoming more prevalent in a variety of applications.
