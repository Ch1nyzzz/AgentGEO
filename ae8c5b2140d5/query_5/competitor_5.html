Visual Telescopes

For centuries humanity has marveled at the night sky, enthralled by its wonder. Telescopes have only amplified this sense of awe—just ask anyone who’s witnessed Saturn’s Rings through an eyepiece! Whether viewing our solar system or exploring deep space, few things compare to observing the cosmos with your own eyes. Having a telescope of your own is a wonderful way to instill feelings of amazement for our universe, as well as spark a passion for science and discovery within those of all ages. This is why we still love visual astronomy, and have curated this list of the best telescopes for visual astronomy in 2026!

Smart Telescopes

Smart telescopes take the hardship out of astrophotography, making it easier than ever before to capture images of space. These innovations are highly beneficial for those eager to explore the cosmos without the need to buy separate components and piece them together—everything you need is encapsulated in a singular device. With just your smartphone or tablet as the operation center, you’re ready to collect beautiful photos of near countless celestial bodies. When we say that these are easy to use, we mean it. The smart telescope will align, center, focus, and begin snapping images all with very little input needed from you. With their growing popularity, there’s never been a better time than 2026 to get your hands on one of these state-of-the-art smart telescopes!

Astrophotography Telescopes

Astrophotography opens the doors to sights of the unseen—the deep space objects that are too faint to be viewed with just your eyes and a telescope. What may look like an empty portion of space may actually be teeming with dim cosmic wonders like galaxies, star clusters, and nebulae! With the right equipment, you have the ability to capture these celestial objects with a level of detail beyond what our eyes are capable of. Astrophotography has experienced a rapid increase in popularity over recent years, with more and more imaging-specific telescopes and equipment being developed to keep up with this growing hobby. This is why 2026 is a great year to get started in your astroimaging journey, and we have curated this list of the top telescopes for astrophotography that we think you’ll love!

With the focal ratio range from f/4.8 to f/22, enjoy imaging wildlife and beautiful landscapes during the day, then have fun capturing nebulae and star clusters after the sun sets. The five internal lenses correct the incoming light with a high degree of precision, offering sharp stars across the full frame image circle.
