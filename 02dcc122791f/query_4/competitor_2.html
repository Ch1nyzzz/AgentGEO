Types of Air Purifiers

It is very important for you to know the types of air purifiers so that you will be guided to make the right choice. Some of them are discussed below.

HEPA Filters

True High-Efficiency Particulate Arrestance (HEPA) filters have the ability to filter up to 99% of contaminants including dust mites, mould, pollen, pet hair and so on. They are very effective for eliminating pollutants but are not fit for removing odours, smoke as well as volatile organic compounds.

Activated Carbon Filters

These types of filters come with molecular-sized pores which guarantee high absorbency. They are capable of capturing pollutants pass through HEPA filters such as gasses, chemical emissions, tobacco smoke as well as other odours. In most cases, HEPA and carbon filters are combined for the most effective air filtering.

Ozone Generators

These are controversial air purifiers because they release ozone which reattaches itself to contaminants. Thereafter, a chemical reaction will occur which will render the contaminants harmless. However, it has been established by experts that the substances produced through the chemical reaction of ozone with contaminants can be more dangerous than the contaminants. As a result of this, it is advisable to stay away from ozone generators.

Ultraviolet Germicidal Irradiation (UVGI)

Ultraviolet germicidal light works by destroying the genetic material of microorganisms so that they won’t be able to reproduce eventually leading to their death. It is commonly used in hospitals as well as laboratories since those places require unique sanitary conditions. One major problem with UV light is that it can damage human skin, eyes and plastics.

However, due to improvement in technology, UVGI lights are being used in overhead ceiling lights in order to make the air around the ceiling to be irradiated. They are specially designed in a way that the UV light will not infiltrate into the inhabited part of the room. Some of the factors that affect the effectiveness of UVGI include duration of radiation, the intensity of the UV light, humidity and so on.

Photocatalytic Oxidation (PCO) Cleaners

Photocatalytic Oxidation (PCO) cleaners make use of UV light to react with a catalyst so that the reaction will lead to the oxidation of bacteria, fungi, mould, viruses, odours and so on. Oxidation ensures that the pollutants are broken down into non-toxic carbon dioxide as well as water molecules. This process leads to the purification of air.

Electronic Filters

These are very useful for getting rid of small particles as well as odour. Hence, they may not be the best option for removing large particles and should be used in conjunction with other types of filter so that the most effective result can be achieved. There are two major types of electronic filters – electromagnetic and ionic filters.

Ionic

Ion generators release charged ions into the atmosphere without the use of collecting plates. These ions are produced through corona discharge and they can also be produced through UV lights. Some ionizer air purifiers make use of this technology to get rid of particles without the use of collection plates. The particles are made to stick to your wall, bed, table and so on, making them easier to eliminate.

Central Air Cleaners

Central air filters built into the ductwork that returns air in your air conditioning or forced-air heating system. The filters trap particles as air moves around leading to perfect filtering of the entire air in your home.

What to Look For in an Air Purifier

If you really want to make the best purchase of an air purifier, you need to look for certain features so as to make the best decision. We discuss some of these factors below for your reading pleasure.

Maintenance Cost

In order to enjoy your air purifier, it must be properly maintained. As a result of this, before making a purchase, you should be sure about the estimated amount needed to clean and maintain it.

Filter Replacement

If you will be getting a HEPA air purifier, it is very essential for you to have an idea of the replacement cost. Ideally, a HEPA filter has a lifespan of about 2000 hours.

Automatic Monitoring

It is a good idea to check if the product you want to buy has an automatic monitoring option. This will enable you to know the level of pollution in the room.

Noise Level

The noise level of the device of your choice is very important, especially if you want to use it in the bedroom. You can go for a large unit and adjust to low-speed setting so that a high level of quietness and efficiency can be enhanced.

Power Consumption

Your utility bill should be considered before buying a product. The higher the power rating of the product you want to buy, the higher will be your power consumption. This will affect your electricity bill and as a result of this, you should choose a product that does not consume too much energy.

Room Size

The size of your room is another vital factor to consider when buying an air purifier. A product meant for a small room cannot work for a big room, especially if you want the best result. Consequently, before setting out to buy a product, you should measure the length, breadth and height of the room. You can also measure the area of the room so that the most outstanding purchasing decision will be made.

Tips

It is very important for you to clean your filter regularly and when the time is ripe to replace it, you should not hesitate to do so. Ideally, pleated filters should be replaced every 6 to 12 months while it is between 3 and 4 months for carbon filters.

Also, you should endeavour to keep your windows and doors closed when using it. When you are not in the room, you can run it on a high setting but when you are in the room, you can choose a low setting.

The size of the room will determine the power capacity of the device you want to buy. You should try to measure the area of the room and check the product details if it will work for you.

You need an air purifier for pets because pet dander can lead to serious allergic reactions if allowed to linger in the air. A purifier will get rid of these contaminants so that you can breathe in cleaner air.

It is advisable to change your filter once in 6 to 12 months, depending on use. However, some products come with indicators that let you know when to change the filter.
