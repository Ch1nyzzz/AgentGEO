Funky Odor Facts: Does An Air Purifier Help With Smell?

If you’re having trouble with funky odor at home, you’ve probably wondered: does an air purifier help with smell? If you’re curious to know how to make your house smell good without candles, air purifiers are one of the best ways to do it.

 

With the help of special filters, known as activated carbon/charcoal, you’ll have a fresher-smelling home in no time. We care about your indoor comfort, as well as the comfort of your guests – no one wants stuffy air at home! That’s why an air purifier will benefit you and your folks at home to feel fresh like no one’s smoked or cooked fried foods.

 

While it’s true that air purifiers are meant mostly for dust, pollen, pet dander, and the like, they can also be used for funky odors, such as those coming from smoke, kitchen odors, mold, VOCs, and the like.

Fact Check: Do air purifiers work for smells?

Yes, they do! Air purifiers aren’t just for dust – they also take out smells with the right filter – the activated carbon/charcoal filter. So, what specific odors will this filter work on? Let’s have a look:

 

  • VOCs – Volatile organic compounds, which are found in most household cleaning items, paints, construction materials, and the like, are harmful to your health. These can be easily cleaned by an air purifier with an activated carbon filter.

 

  • Kitchen odors – If the biggest problem in your home is leftover food smell or even fried food then air purifiers will help you out. Kitchen odors will subside once you start running your air purifier with an activated carbon filter – just make sure it’s placed properly in your kitchen without blockage.

 

  • Pet odors – Fido and Tabby might like to sit around the couch and your guest(s) probably don’t like pet smells. A solution to that is to use an air purifier with an activated carbon filter to absorb such pet odors.

 

  • Tobacco smoke – Do you or your companions at home smoke? Do guests smoke? If so, an air purifier will help keep the hazardous smoke smell to a minimum – your lungs will thank you later.

 

 

Will an air purifier help with the urine smell?

Yes, air purifiers will help with urine smells, typically for pet accidents. However, this isn’t a complete replacement for physical cleaning! Make sure that you still scrub the affected area with baking soda and vinegar to get rid of the smell entirely.

 

After that, an air purifier with an activated carbon/charcoal filter will take care of the rest. Just make sure to check regularly if your carbon filter is still okay to use as per the manufacturer’s instructions (or if it needs to be replaced).

 

Will an air purifier help with old house smell?

If your air purifier has an activated charcoal/carbon filter then yes, it will help with a funky and stuffy old house smell. Therefore, if you’ve just moved into a previously unoccupied space, it’s best to use an air purifier with that kind of filter to make the air less stuffy.

 

The reason an air purifier is effective against old house smells is the mold-combating properties of activated carbon. Aside from manually scrubbing away molded areas of the house or room, air purifiers keep the mold away due to this carbon filter, but we also recommend having a dehumidifier to balance the room’s humidity and keep mold at bay.

 

Conclusion

To wrap it up, air purifiers do help with unwanted smells in your indoor space. Whether it’s fried food, leftovers, tobacco smoke, pet smells, VOCs, and others, you’ll have a fresher-smelling home if you continue using an air purifier with an activated carbon filter (and maintaining it properly). We hope this article cleared out some information about how air purifiers fight bad smells!
