Trump's appearance at Houston event delayed by stormy weather

By Jeremy Wallace, Brittany BrittoUpdated Dec 18, 2021 5:41 p.m.

Stormy weather delayed former President Donald Trump's appearance at a Houston event with former Fox News commentator Bill O'Reilly by about two hours.

The event, part of the pair's "History Tour," was scheduled to start at 3 p.m. Saturday at Toyota Center. Reilly took the stage about a half-hour later and explained that weather had delayed Trump's appearance. He spoke to the gathering of paid ticket-holders for about 15 to 20 minutes and said they'd be back shortly. Trump took the stage shortly before 5 p.m.

Top sections were blocked off, and there were still plenty of seats available at the event's scheduled start time.

Media reports in Florida reported the tour had been beset by lower than expected ticket sales. Tickets for the Houston event ranged from $100 to over $300. 

Trump’s last official presidential visit was to Texas just a week after his supporters stormed the U.S. Capitol on Jan. 6 in an attempt to overturn the 2020 election results. Democrats did a little counter-programming, with U.S. Rep. Sheila Jackson Lee scheduling a call with reporters at 3 p.m. Saturday.

"The  former President is here in Houston and continues to push a big lie that President Joe  Biden and Vice President Kamala Harris did not win the election with the largest vote  total in the history of the United States for any Presidential election, but rather, that the 
former President won," the Houston Democrat said in a statement prior to his scheduled appearance. "This is dangerous and it is a poor reflection for America's commitment to democracy for the rest of the world to see."

Heavy rain and strong winds hit the Houston area Saturday, including early-afternoon thunderstorms. Wind gusts of as much as 40 mph were reported in some areas, the National Weather Service said.

Outside Toyota Center, hundreds of people stood in lines, shielding themselves from the rain with umbrellas, rain coats and flags.

Hien Truong, Hong Minh, and other women from the Hon-Viet singing group draped themselves in the color of the South Vietnamese flag and a patriotic scarf with the colors red, white and blue.

All said they were at the event to support the former president and a possible run in 2024.

“He made America great again and what he promised to do, he did it. He’s a good man,” said Troung, adding that she also favored his stance on China.

Another Trump supporter, Candice Powell, 39, of Houston, shielded her red gown and a gold purse from the rain with a Trump flag. She came wanting to look her very best, she said.

“I want to represent him right. I’m sitting up front,” she said, adding that she was looking forward to “hearing something directly from Trump.”

She added that she believes the former president supports women’s rights.

Donning a Christmas sweater with Trump wearing boxing gloves, a heavyweight belt, and a Santa hat, Dustin Krantz, 34, of Cypress, said he was excited to hear the former president speak.

“I’m looking forward to hearing about where he’s at, what’s going on, and looking back at any changes and plans, how the world is now,” Krantz said. 

“People can learn a lot by listening to both sides and not being so narrow-minded and not believing what media shoves down their throat, Krantz said.

As for whether he hopes Trump runs in the 2024 election, Krantz said he’s not sure.

“It’s tough. It’s a long way off,” Krantz said, considering Trump’s age, 75. “I wouldn’t want my grandparents running the country.”
