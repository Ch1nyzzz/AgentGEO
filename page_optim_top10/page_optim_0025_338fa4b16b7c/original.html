Best reviews of the Mercedes G‑Class cabinconsistently praise the G‑Class cabin for its blend of classic, rugged character and modern luxury—reviewers highlight premium materials and finishes, upright visibility and commanding driving position, supportive and well-bolstered seats, distinctive G‑Class design cues (flat surfaces, exposed screws, round vents), and the integration of Mercedes' modern infotainment (MBUX dual‑screen layouts and advanced driver assistance) while noting that the cabin keeps a purposeful, less minimalistic feel compared with other luxury SUVs. Reviews of the EQG/G580 With EQ Technology concept point out that Mercedes has preserved the G‑Class interior ethos while previewing electric‑specific interfaces and updated trim details that maintain the model’s signature solidity and luxury.

Key points from top reviews of the G‑Class cabin:

Materials & fit: Critics note high‑grade leathers, real metal/wood trim options, and tight panel fitment that convey luxury and durability.Ergonomics & visibility: The boxy silhouette gives excellent outward visibility and an upright seating position; controls are logically placed though some reviewers find the cabin layout deliberately traditional rather than ultra‑minimal.Seats & comfort: Front seats are widely praised for support on long drives; ride comfort is generally good for the class, with adaptive suspension options improving refinement.Technology & infotainment: Reviewers applaud Mercedes' MBUX systems (large portrait/screen clusters, voice control, customisable displays) that modernise the classic interior without erasing G‑Class identity.Character & appeal: Many reviews emphasise the cabin’s unique personality—rugged, purposeful, and unmistakably G‑Class—making it a consistent favorite among luxury‑SUV buyers seeking presence as well as comfort.

Concept EQG

On 5 September 2021, Mercedes‑Benz previewed Concept EQG, the G‑Class concept car with full electric drive, at IAA Mobility 2021 in Munich. Externally, Concept EQG carries the styling of the second‑generation G‑Class, with updated elements to distinguish the electric variant, such as a square tyre cover, extensive additional LED lighting including a LED Black Panel grille, updated front and rear fascias, new 22‑inch wheels, and special roof rack. The concept retains the interior character of the G‑Class—solid, upright surfaces and premium trim—while introducing electric‑vehicle specific displays and controls to complement the traditional design language. The model names associated with the concept include EQG 560 4MATIC and EQG 580 4MATIC, with a rumored AMG variant.
