WHAT IS LEUKEMIA?

It is a cancer which affects the blood cells mainly white blood cells. White blood cells are the component of blood that provide energy to the body to fight against infections. When a person suffers from leukemia, the mutation of DNA occurs and results in production of large number of immature white blood cells, called Blasts. Leukemia affects the different type of cell in the blood and accordingly it is divided into different types: Acute Myeloid leukemia (AML), chronic lymphocytic leukemia (CLL) & Hairy cell leukemia (HCL).

AYURVEDIC VIEW

According to Ayurveda, blood is considered as Jiva (life) – ‘रक्तो जीव इति स्थिति:’ blood is one of dhatu (body tissues) among 7 dhatu. Ayurveda mentions that oxygen is carried by blood cells about thousands of years ago- ‘प्राणा: शोणितं अनुवर्तन्ते’.

As per Ayurveda Blood disorders are due to pitta imbalance and Blood Cancers can also be classified as “dysfunctional Pitta” leading faulty formation / damage of blood cells or immunity cells.

SYMPTOMS OF LEUKEMIA

  • Poor blood clotting
  • Frequent infections
  • Weak immunity
  • Anemia
  • Swollen lymph nodes, enlarged liver or spleen
  • Easy bleeding or bruising
  • Recurrent nose bleeding
  • Tiny red spots on the skin (petechiae)
  • Excessive sweating at night
  • Other symptoms such as nausea, fever, chills, night sweats, flu-like symptoms, weight loss, bone pain, and tiredness.

CAUSES OF LEUKEMIA

The exact cause of leukemia is not known but it may cause due to combination of environmental and genetic factors. It is due to the Mutation in DNA that results in abnormal production of WBC. But the cause of mutation is not known.

Other causes may be Carcinogens such as tobacco, asbestos, arsenic, radiation, ultraviolet rays and car exhaust fumes.

Alcohol use and obesity, Lack of physical activity, Sunlight and excessive exposure to ultraviolet light and Exposure to radiation from X-rays, gamma rays, cellular phones and cell phone towers.

RISK FACTORS OF LEUKEMIA

Certain genetic disorders like Down syndrome, Li-Fraumeni syndrome and other disease conditions such as myelodysplastic syndromes (blood disorder) increase the risk of leukemia.

Exposure to certain chemicals, including benzene (commonly used in the chemical industry), increases the risk of leukemia. Cigarette smoking is known to increase the risk by developing AML.
